ABOUT ME

I search for connections between patterns and the details driving those patterns, whether in biology or data science. My desire to know both the how and the why of the world around me drives my ability to solve problems in a creative yet methodical manner.

After completing a research internship in neuroscience at Georgetown's Medical Center, I decided to pursue an undergraduate degree in biology. I took a broad range of courses within the Biology department, becoming especially fascinated with bioinformatics, evolutionary biology, and microbiology. Georgetown's focus on encouraging students to develop a strong liberal arts foundation allowed me to also pursue my interests in philosophy, English, and international affairs.

After graduating with a degree in biology and a desire to apply my knowledge in a research setting, I realized I would need to hone my technical skills before finding a career I both enjoyed and found meaningful. I chose General Assembly's Data Science Immersive program for its use of Python, fast pace, and high-pressure environment. I have loved every minute of it, and want to apply my new skills and keep learning wherever I can do the most good.
